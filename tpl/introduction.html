I-DECIDE – A healthy relationship and safety decision aid website for women

Introducing the project

Many women in Australia sometimes feel unsafe in their relationships, or worry that their relationships with their partners are not healthy. We are conducting a project to test a website that could increase knowledge about healthy relationships, improve safety planning, and help women make decisions when they are afraid of a partner or ex-partner.

We are inviting women (between 16 and 50 years of age) who have experienced relationship issues over the last 6 months and have safe computer and internet access to take part in this project. It is important that you carefully read this information before you make a decision to participate.

Who are the researchers?

This project is being conducted by a team led by Kelsey Hegarty who is a general practitioner with an interest in women’s health. The project is based at the Department of General Practice at the University of Melbourne. It is funded through the Australian Research Council.

What will I be asked to do?

If you are eligible, a computer will randomly assign you to one of two groups: the ‘Resource’ group or the ‘Resource and Intervention group’. This means you will be assigned to a group by chance, like flipping a coin. You will receive an email with a link to a website, where you can log in with a secure username and password. You can access the website anytime, 24 hours a day, throughout the 12 months of the project. At the end of 12 months, women in the ‘Resource’ group will also have access to the intervention.

Whether you are in the ‘Resource’ group or the ‘Resource and Intervention’ group, you will be asked a series of questions about you and your partner and about your relationship and receive advice on safety planning and resources. The ‘Resource and Intervention’ group will also receive individual feedback based on risk and priorities. We expect it might take you up to an hour to complete everything, but you don’t have to do it all at once. After you have finished, you will receive a $40 Coles/Myer gift card. Six months later, you will be asked to do another short survey online, and you will receive a $50 Coles/Myer gift card. Twelve months after you first accessed the website, you will be asked to complete a final short survey, and you will receive a $60 Coles/Myer gift card.

Your safety and privacy

Because your safety is vital, it is essential that you are able to access a computer and the internet in a safe, private environment (for example at a family member or friend’s place). The website contains an emergency exit feature that allows you to leave with one mouse click.

Your responses and any information you give us are confidential (subject to legal requirements). Only the research team will have access. The project data and records will be kept for a minimum of five years after publication, or public release, of the work of the project. It will be then destroyed according to University guidelines.
